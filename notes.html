Elasticity, Hooke's Law & Modulus Guide

Welcome to the comprehensive guide on elasticity. These notes cover everything from simple spring behavior to the structural properties of materials.

1. Hooke's Law

Hooke's law states that the extension of a spring is directly proportional to the applied force, provided that the elastic limit is not exceeded.

F = k × e

Where:

  • F = Force applied (Newtons, N)
  • k = Spring Constant (Stiffness) (N/m)
  • e = Extension (meters, m)
Critical Student Note

In Physics, mass (kg) is not the same as Force. To convert mass to Force (Weight), multiply by gravity (approx. 9.8 N/kg or 10 N/kg depending on the syllabus).

F = m × g

2. Spring Constant (k)

The spring constant measures how stiff an individual spring is. A higher k means a stiffer spring.

k =
F
e
Example: Finding k

A mass of 200g is hung from a spring, causing it to extend by 100mm. Find its spring constant (g = 10 N/kg).

  1. Convert Mass to Force: 200g = 0.2kg. F = 0.2 × 10 = 2N.
  2. Convert Extension to meters: 100mm = 0.1m.
  3. Calculate: k = 2N / 0.1m = 20 N/m.

3. Springs in Parallel

When two identical springs are placed side-by-side (parallel) to support a load, they share the weight. This makes the system twice as stiff.

ktotal = k1 + k2

5. The Stress-Strain Curve

A graph of Stress vs. Strain reveals material limits:

  • Proportional Limit: Linear region where Hooke's Law is obeyed.
  • Elastic Limit / Yield Point: Point where permanent deformation begins.
  • Ultimate Tensile Stress: Maximum stress the material can withstand.
  • Fracture Point: Where the material physically snaps.

Practice Questions

Q1: A spring extends by 10cm when a mass of 100g is attached. What is its spring constant? (g = 10 N/kg)

F = 0.1kg × 10 = 1N. e = 0.1m.
k = 1N / 0.1m = 10 N/m.

Q2: A wire of length 2.0m and area 5.0 × 10-7 m² experiences a stress of 4.0 × 107 Pa. Calculate the applied force.

F = σ × A = (4.0 × 107) × (5.0 × 10-7) = 20 N.

Q3: Calculate the strain if a 25cm wire stretches to 25.7cm.

ΔL = 0.7cm. L0 = 25cm.
Strain = 0.7 / 25 = 0.028.
